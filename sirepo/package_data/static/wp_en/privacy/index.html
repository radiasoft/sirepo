Use of Cookies and Similar Tracking Technologies

Our Service may use cookies, web beacons, pixel tags, and similar digital tracking technologies, such as Google Analytics. Cookies are small data files that are transferred to your hard drive when you visit a website if your browser settings permit the acceptance of cookies. Similar technologies, such as web beacons or pixel tags, function through the use of small files, such as a GIF or PNG, which are opened on a page, ad, or in an email. The request for these files from a server reveals certain information about your device, such as your browser type, device information (such as OS version, device model), IP address, etc. These technologies can also be used to determine how you interact with our Service, emails, or ads, such as to tell us whether an email or ad was viewed, opened, or clicked, and other similar information. To learn more about cookies and similar tracking technologies, and how they can affect your privacy, visit allaboutcookies.org.

We typically use cookies and similar technologies on our Service for the following purposes:

  • For “analytics” purposes, to learn how our Service is used or performs, how users engage with and navigate our Service, how often they visit our Service, and other similar information.

Please note, most modern browsers include a “do not track” feature that may limit the information collected by these technologies. However, our Service does not currently respond to your browser’s do-not-track request.
